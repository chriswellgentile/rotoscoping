Multimedia and Authoring

About the Course

Where motion meets art — and rotoscoping bridges the hand-drawn with the digital world.

What is Multimedia and Authoring?

Multimedia and Authoring (MMA) is a dynamic course that covers the full spectrum of digital creative production — from concept development and storyboarding to 3D modeling, animation, and post-production.

The course challenges students to think visually, communicate through motion, and master industry-standard tools like Blender, Adobe After Effects, and Photoshop.

Rotoscoping is one of the core techniques explored — a process that dates back to the golden age of animation and remains deeply relevant in modern VFX pipelines.

🎬

Multimedia and Authoring bridges traditional animation techniques with modern digital workflows, creating a foundation for careers in film, games, and interactive media.

✏️

Rotoscoping involves tracing over live-action footage frame by frame to produce realistic animation — a technique used by Disney, Ralph Bakshi, and modern studios alike.

What is Rotoscoping?

Rotoscoping is the animation technique of tracing over motion picture footage, frame by frame, to produce realistic action. Originally invented by Max Fleischer in 1917, it was patented as the "Rotoscope."

In this course, we apply rotoscoping to 5-second clips per week over 12 weeks — building a cumulative 60-second animation that demonstrates mastery of timing, line quality, and motion study.

The technique directly informs 3D animation by training the eye to read motion, weight, and silhouette — essential skills whether working in 2D or in Blender.

Connection to 3D Animation

Rotoscoping and 3D animation are deeply intertwined. Understanding how real movement translates to keyframes is foundational to rigging and animating 3D characters in Blender. The 3D Solar System project in this course applies those same principles — studying light, form, and motion at a planetary scale.
